**Research Approach**: Citizen Science (CBPR - Community-Based Participatory Research)

**Study Area**: Two lake basins in Michoac√°n, Mexico
- Ci√©nega de Chapala basin
- P√°tzcuaro-Zirahu√©n basin

**Participants**:
- 26 Citizen Scientists (young university students)

- Monte Carlo simulation methods

## üìä Key Findings

### Risk Map by Region

The study generated a comprehensive risk assessment across multiple indicators including:
- Biological susceptibility
- Social deficiency (education, healthcare access, health coverage)
- Prevalence of diseases
- Pest management practices
- Impacts on biodiversity
- Regulatory framework
- Human resource generation

**Conclusion**: The situation is undesirable, as most risk indicators place populations in both regions at the highest level of risk.

### Risk Perception

"There is insufficient and inadequate risk perception by agricultural producers and the general population" - Main conclusion from Citizen Scientists.

Producers are torn between recognizing the risk and trying to protect themselves, or definitively minimizing exposure risks to agrochemicals with various arguments.
